# Benefits to People with Disabilities & Society — Verified Research

## Disability Population Statistics

- **70+ million U.S. adults (28.7%)** have some type of disability (2022 BRFSS)
  - Source: [CDC 2024](https://www.cdc.gov/media/releases/2024/s0716-Adult-disability.html)
- **12.2% of adults** have a mobility disability (difficulty walking/climbing stairs) — 2nd most common type
  - Source: [CDC Infographic](https://www.cdc.gov/disability-and-health/articles-documents/disability-impacts-all-of-us-infographic.html)
- **4+ million Californians** have a disability; ~5.8% (nearly 2M) have ambulatory disabilities
  - Source: [CA HCD](https://www.hcd.ca.gov/policy-and-research/intersectional-policy-work/people-disabilities)
- **30.6 million** had difficulty walking/climbing stairs or used wheelchair/cane/crutches/walker (2010)
  - Source: [Census Bureau 2010](https://www.census.gov/newsroom/releases/archives/miscellaneous/cb12-134.html)
- **3.6-6 million wheelchair users** in the U.S.
  - Source: [Census Bureau](https://www.census.gov/newsroom/releases/archives/miscellaneous/cb12-134.html)

## Impact of Physical Barriers on Daily Life

- People with disabilities are **3.14x more likely to experience loneliness** — driven by environmental barriers, not disability itself
  - Source: [PMC/Disability and Health Journal](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC7403030/)
- Environmental barriers (inaccessible spaces, transport) are primary loneliness driver — having a job or barrier-free environment are protective factors
  - Source: [Frontiers in Psychology 2023](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC9905422/)
- **25% of disabled people** encounter barriers "often or always" at retail entrances; 29% "sometimes"
  - Source: [Return on Disability Group](https://www.rod-group.com/research-insights/barriers-for-in-person-retail-getting-past-the-entrance/)
- **1 in 4 working-age adults** with disabilities lack a usual healthcare provider; 1 in 4 have unmet needs due to cost
  - Source: [CDC Infographic](https://www.cdc.gov/disability-and-health/articles-documents/disability-impacts-all-of-us-infographic.html)
- Women with disabilities: 61.4% got mammograms vs 74.4% without; men 19% less likely for prostate screening
  - Source: [ADA National Network](https://adata.org/research_brief/research-brief-health-care-access-and-ada)

## Economic Impact / Disability Market

- **$490 billion** in after-tax disposable income for working-age adults with disabilities
  - Source: [American Institutes for Research](https://www.air.org/resource/report/hidden-market-purchasing-power-working-age-adults-disabilities)
- **$175 billion** in discretionary spending (more than 4x the tween market)
  - Source: [ADA.gov / DOL](https://archive.ada.gov/busstat.htm)
- **$50 billion/year** in travel spending by disabled travelers; $100B+ including companions
  - Source: [Open Doors Org / Harris Poll 2024](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/50-billion-in-annual-spending-highlights-impact-of-travelers-with-disabilities-302321017.html)
- **$6.9 trillion** globally in disability disposable income (2.3B people)
  - Source: [W3C WAI](https://www.w3.org/WAI/business-case/)
- Companies leading in disability inclusion: **28% higher revenue**, 2x net income, 30% higher profit margins
  - Source: [Accenture 2018](https://newsroom.accenture.com/news/2018/companies-leading-in-disability-inclusion-have-outperformed-peers-accenture-research-finds)
- U.S. GDP could gain **up to $25 billion** from increased disability employment
  - Source: [Accenture](https://newsroom.accenture.com/news/2018/companies-leading-in-disability-inclusion-have-outperformed-peers-accenture-research-finds)

## Employment Gap

- **22.7% employment rate** for disabled people vs 65.5% without — 43-point gap (2024)
  - Source: [BLS Feb 2025](https://www.bls.gov/news.release/disabl.htm)
- Workers with disability nearly 2x as likely to work part-time
  - Source: [BLS](https://www.bls.gov/news.release/disabl.htm)
- Inaccessible workplaces reduce employment opportunities for mobility device users
  - Source: [Taylor & Francis 2022](https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/full/10.1080/17483107.2022.2111723)

## Aging Population

- **61.2 million Americans 65+** (18% of population, 2024) — up 34% from 2012
  - Projected: 82M by 2050, 95M by 2060
  - Source: [Census Bureau 2025](https://www.census.gov/newsroom/press-releases/2025/older-adults-outnumber-children.html)
  - Source: [PRB](https://www.prb.org/resource/fact-sheet-aging-in-the-united-states/)
- **44% of people 65+** report a disability; ~35% of those 70+ have mobility limitations
  - Source: [CDC 2024](https://www.cdc.gov/media/releases/2024/s0716-Adult-disability.html)
  - Source: [BMC Geriatrics](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10360303/)
- 65+ population grew **13% from 2020-2024** vs just 1.4% for working-age adults
  - Source: [Census Bureau](https://www.census.gov/newsroom/press-releases/2025/older-adults-outnumber-children.html)

## Congressional Intent of the ADA

- Congress found discrimination through "architectural, transportation, and communication barriers"
  - Source: [42 USC 12101](https://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/42/12101)
- Found disabled people "occupy an inferior status in our society" and are "severely disadvantaged socially, vocationally, economically, and educationally"
  - Source: [42 USC 12101](https://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/42/12101)
- Purpose: "clear and comprehensive national mandate for the elimination of discrimination"
  - Source: [ADA.gov](https://www.ada.gov/law-and-regs/ada/)

## Why Technology-Assisted Compliance Helps

- ADA Standards include "equivalent facilitation" to accommodate "good faith innovations and technological advances"
  - Source: [U.S. Access Board](https://www.access-board.gov/ada/guides/chapter-1-using-the-ada-standards/)
- American Foundation for the Blind recommends AI "validation and auditing practices"
  - Source: [AFB 2025](https://afb.org/news-publications/press-room/press-release-archive/press-release-2025/afb-report-spotlights-impact-AI-disabled-people)
- Only 46% of healthcare admins knew accessible equipment existed; 22% knew about tax credits
  - Source: [ADA National Network](https://adata.org/research_brief/research-brief-health-care-access-and-ada)
