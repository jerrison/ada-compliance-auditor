# ADA Compliance Triggers — Verified Research

## 1. No Grandfather Clause — Ongoing Barrier Removal

- **42 USC 12182(b)(2)(A)(iv)**: failure to remove architectural barriers in existing facilities where removal is "readily achievable" is discrimination
- Statute: [42 USC 12182](https://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/42/12182)
- **"Readily achievable" defined** at 42 USC 12181(9): "easily accomplishable without much difficulty or expense"
- Statute: [42 USC 12181](https://uscode.house.gov/view.xhtml?req=(title:42+section:12181+edition:prelim))
- **28 CFR 36.304**: Implementing regulation with 4 priorities: (1) access from sidewalks/parking; (2) access to goods/services; (3) restrooms; (4) other
- Regulation: [28 CFR 36.304](https://www.law.cornell.edu/cfr/text/28/36.304)
- **Ongoing obligation**: must periodically re-evaluate
- ADA.gov: "Almost all types of businesses... regardless of size or age of buildings, must follow the ADA"
- Source: [ADA.gov Title III](https://www.ada.gov/topics/title-iii/)
- Explainer: [Burnham Nationwide](https://www.burnhamnationwide.com/final-review-blog/a-misunderstood-area-of-ada-compliance-existing-facilities)

## 2. New Construction (post-1993)

- All facilities first occupied after January 26, 1993 must comply with ADA Accessibility Guidelines
- Source: [Maynard Nexsen](https://www.maynardnexsen.com/publication-ada-compliance-in-real-estate)

## 3. CBC 11B-202.4 — The 20% Path-of-Travel Rule

- When alterations/additions/structural repairs are made, accessible path of travel to the altered area must be provided
- Path of travel includes: primary entrance, restrooms, drinking fountains, telephones, signage
- Cost limited to 20% of adjusted construction cost
- **2026 valuation threshold: $209,208** (effective Jan 19, 2026)
- DSA source: [DSA Valuation Threshold 2026](https://www.dgs.ca.gov/DSA/News/Page-Content/Division-of-the-State-Architect-News-List-Folder/Valuation-Threshold-Update-for-2026)
- Code text: [UpCodes 11B-202.4](https://up.codes/s/path-of-travel-requirements-in-alterations-additions-and-structural-repairs)
- **Three-year lookback**: costs from alterations within 3 years combine
- **Priority order** when budget limited: (1) entrance, (2) route, (3) restrooms, (4) telephones, (5) fountains, (6) parking/signs/storage
- Source: [Proactive Access](https://www.proactiveaccess.com/casp-blog/valuation-threshold)

## 4. Tenant Improvements — Same 20% Rule

- Any alteration to a primary function area triggers 11B-202.4
- Both landlord and tenant jointly liable
- Code: [UpCodes 11B-202.4](https://up.codes/s/path-of-travel-requirements-in-alterations-additions-and-structural-repairs)
- UC Berkeley guidance: [DAC Valuation Threshold](https://dac.berkeley.edu/services/architectural-accessibility/valuation-threshold)
- Joint liability: [Accessibility.com](https://www.accessibility.com/blog/who-is-responsible-for-ada-compliance-in-commercial-buildings) (citing Botosan v. Fitzhugh)

## 5. SF Accessible Business Entrance (ABE) Program

- Ordinance 51-16 (2016): required accessible primary entrances for pre-2002 buildings
- **Program sunsetted April 7, 2025** via Ordinance No. 22-25
- ~7,000 businesses citywide remained noncompliant at sunset
- Businesses still must comply with CBC and ADA
- SF offers Accessible Barrier Removal Grants up to $10,000
- Source: [SF.gov ABE Program](https://www.sf.gov/accessible-business-entrance-abe-program)
- Source: [SF Public Press](https://www.sfpublicpress.org/sf-business-entrance-accessibility-law-never-enforced-to-be-scrapped/)
- Post-sunset requirements: [SF.gov Accessibility Requirements](https://www.sf.gov/accessibility-requirements-in-san-francisco)
- Architecture firm details: [Hilliard Architects](https://www.hilliardarchitects.com/services/sfabe)

## 6. SB 1608 / Qualified Defendant Status

- **Cal. Civ. Code 55.52**: "qualified defendant" = CASp inspection before being served
- Statute: [Justia Cal. Civ. Code 55.52](https://law.justia.com/codes/california/code-civ/division-1/part-2-52/section-55-52/)
- **Cal. Civ. Code 55.56**: Damages reduced from $4,000 to $1,000 minimum if CASp-inspected + violations corrected within 60 days
- Statute: [Justia Cal. Civ. Code 55.56](https://law.justia.com/codes/california/code-civ/division-1/part-2-53/section-55-56/)
- 90-day stay and Early Evaluation Conference available
- **SB 269 120-day grace period**: businesses ≤50 employees get 120 days from CASp inspection
- DSA PDF: [DSA Notice of Access Inspection](https://www.dgs.ca.gov/-/media/Divisions/DSA/Publications/casp/Notice-of-Access-Inspect-120-Day-Period.pdf)
- Source: [ADA Certified](https://www.adacertified.com/what-is-casp)

## 7. Property Transfer

- ADA obligations attach to the property, not the previous owner
- Joint and several liability for landlord and tenant
- Cannot contract away ADA liability (Botosan v. Paul McNally Realty, 216 F.3d 827, 9th Cir. 2000)
- Case: [Justia Botosan](https://law.justia.com/cases/federal/appellate-courts/F3/216/827/570426/)
- Federal civil penalties: $75K first / $150K subsequent
- Source: [Maynard Nexsen](https://www.maynardnexsen.com/publication-ada-compliance-in-real-estate)

## CASp Inspector Shortage

- ~900 CASp inspectors for 4.1M CA businesses (industry estimate, not official DSA count)
- 39% exam pass rate (industry-cited, not verifiable from DSA)
- 4-8 week wait times in SF, LA, San Diego
- Costs: $750-$2,400 small/medium; up to $25K for multi-tenant
- SF lists only 9 local CASp specialists
